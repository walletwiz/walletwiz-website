Ages 5 - 7

Duration: 60 minutes

This is the perfect age to introduce students to the concept of money! Through fun games and activities, students will explore the following topics:

  • What is Money?
  • Identifying currency (coins & bills)
  • Piggy bank savings

Ages 8 - 12

Duration: 60 minutes

With students having instant access to information and commerce online, this is the right age to develop a strong understanding of what personal finance means. In this interactive session, students will explore:

  • Understanding the value of money
  • Piggy bank savings (advanced)
  • Understanding bank accounts
  • Financial goal setting

Ages 13 - 17

Duration: 90 minutes

Students in high school have an increased level of independence in their lives and this is reflected in their personal finance journeys as well. Whether it is managing a lunch money allowance, getting a new part time job, or wanting to save up for a major purchase - it is imperative that students are making smart financial decisions to lay the foundation for their futures. In this session, students will explore:

  • Budgeting
  • Understanding and making purchases
  • Understanding e-commerce & Online Shopping
  • Bank accounts and debit cards
  • Introduction to credit
  • Introduction to investment
  • Financing post secondary education

Ages 18 - 24

Duration: 120 minutes

At this age, it is imperative for people to have a strong understanding of personal finance fundamentals. Once you turn 18, you now qualify for credit, are able to invest your money, and truly take advantage of the facilities of the Canadian finance system. You also likely have increased financial responsibilities in your life, and that is why this course aims to equip you with the tools you need to succeed! In this course you will explore:

  • Budgeting
  • Student loans and debt management
  • Credit fundamentals
  • Investment fundamentals
  • Bank accounts, debit cards, and deposits
